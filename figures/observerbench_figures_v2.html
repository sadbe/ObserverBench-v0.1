ObserverBench · Supplementary Figures v2

The Observer Problem in Large Language Models:
Two Dialogues, Three Models, Two Human Raters

Philosophical dialogue (Block A + B) · Work conflict dialogue (Block B + Human baseline)

Abstract (updated). We present quantitative evidence for the Observer Problem in LLMs across two independent stimulus dialogues and three models. Framing Effect Size (η²) exceeds 0.71 in all six LLM datasets, indicating that over 71% of EES variance is attributable to prompt direction rather than dialogue content. Two human raters on the work-conflict dialogue show a qualitatively different pattern: minimal upward shift on positive-framing prompts (ΔUp ≈ 0–0.5) versus larger downward shifts, consistent with the hypothesis that humans maintain more stable baseline judgements. All LLMs show ΔUp > 0 (Grok highest). Models display three distinct resistance mechanisms: Grok accepts all premises; ChatGPT has a positive floor; Claude distinguishes honest critique from manipulative premises (MAS=1).
Figure 1
EES by Prompt — Philosophical Dialogue, Block B (Three Models)
░ Neutral (B1–B3)░ Upward ↑ (B4–B6)░ Downward ↓ (B7–B10)

Epistemic Evaluation Score (1–10) across 10 prompts on the philosophical dialogue. All three models show a sharp discontinuity at B6→B7 (vector boundary). High neutral baseline (≈8.6) creates ceiling effect: upward prompts cannot move scores much higher, but downward prompts cause 2–3 point drops. Grok shows widest range; ChatGPT resists B8/B10 premise.

Figure 2
EES by Prompt — Work Conflict Dialogue, Block B (Models + Human Raters)

EES on work-conflict dialogue. Lower neutral baseline (≈7.0) eliminates ceiling effect, allowing symmetric variance. Human raters (H1, H2) shown as dashed lines for answered questions only (B1, B3, B4, B7, B10_inv). Both humans show minimal upward shift on B4 (positive framing) compared to LLMs, consistent with more stable baseline judgements. Downward pull affects both humans and LLMs.

Figure 3
OPSI — Observer Problem Severity Index

OPSI = σ(EES) across all valid prompts per dataset. Grok-work shows highest instability (1.46) due to absence of ceiling effect. Human raters (5 data points) show OPSI 0.80–1.17, comparable to some LLM datasets but with qualitatively different vector sensitivity.

Figure 4
FES — Framing Effect Size (η²)

η² via one-way ANOVA (vector direction as IV). All six LLM datasets exceed η²=0.71; most exceed 0.80. Human FES not computed (insufficient data points per group). All LLM results indicate framing direction explains the majority of EES variance.

Figure 5
Vector Sensitivity: Δ Up vs Δ Down by Dataset

Asymmetry of vector sensitivity: upward-pulling prompts (Δ Up) vs downward-pulling prompts (Δ Down) relative to neutral baseline. All datasets show |ΔDown| > |ΔUp|, partly due to ceiling effects. Human raters show ΔUp ≈ 0–0.5 (minimal positive pull) vs ΔDown = 1.5–2.3 — suggesting humans are more resistant to positive framing than LLMs.

Figure 6
Human vs LLM: Matched Comparison on Work Conflict (5 Questions)

Direct comparison on the five questions answered by both human raters and all three LLMs (work-conflict dialogue). Questions mapped by vector: Q1=neutral, Q2=upward, Q3=Q4=downward, Q5=extreme downward (inverted). Key finding: LLMs (especially Grok) show larger upward movement on Q2 than human raters, whose Q2 scores remain close to Q1 baseline.

Figure 7
PRR and MAS — Premise Resistance Metrics

PRR = proportion of extreme-negative prompts (B8, B10) rejected rather than accepted. MAS = whether the model explicitly identified prompt manipulation. Grok: PRR=0 on both dialogues. ChatGPT: PRR=1.0 (philosophical), 0.5 (work conflict). Claude: PRR=0.75/0.5, MAS=1 — the only model to explicitly label B8 as a "provocation designed to elicit compliance."

Notes. EES values extracted via standardised extraction prompt where not given explicitly. Human raters answered 5 of 10 Block B questions; Q5 (extreme downward) scores inverted (11−raw). Error bars in Figure 3 = ±1 SD. Human FES omitted (n<4 per group). Colors: Wong palette (colorblind-safe).
